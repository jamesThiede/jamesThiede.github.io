While I am almost certain that I am forgetting a few things, I think this list is certainly a good start.

Meditation

Woo! You may be aware of the fact that I am all about the meditation life. I am not sure why this hasn't helped, probably due to not setting up a set schedule and sticking to it.

Duration Effort Required Consistency
About 2 weeks 7/10 - Meditation takes serious dedication! ~4 nights a week
  • Why I Started - I like meditation and wanted to explore the potential benefits it could have for sleep
  • Why I Stopped - Was not dedicated enough
  • What I Liked - Meditation! Also, its healthy for a variety of reasons to quiet your mind every once in a while
  • What I Disliked - Was challenging to bring myself to do it if I am/was super tired

Not using electronics X number of hours before bed

Science says that its bad to look at screens before bed. I can neither confirm nor deny that this technique works as I am hopelessly addicted to electronics. It also doesn't help that I spend a minimum of 8 hours a day staring at a screen for work.

Duration Effort Required Consistency
Embarrassingly Short 11/10 - I am not proud of my addiction to technology but I can't say it doesn't exist Shhhh...
  • Why I Started - Numerous studies say how awful it is to stare at a screen before bed
  • Why I Stopped - It is very difficult to find something to do in bed when you can't sleep that doesn't involve a screen
  • What I Liked - Nothing
  • What I Disliked - Everything

Reading

A good alternative to staring at my computer or phone. Reading tends to wake my brain up so it is kind of counter-productive.

Duration Effort Required Consistency
Longest period was only about three weeks consistently 8/10 - I love reading but I am not good at it, will explain later (I promise I know how to read) 2-3 nights a week (Would only do on weeknights)
  • Why I Started - Saw this as a good alternative to being on my phone or computer
  • Why I Stopped - I have a difficult time focusing when reading, and have a difficult time doing anything when I'm tired (Ironically I have difficulty sleeping when I'm tired lol). Also, I am terrible at finding good books to read *insert opportunity to recommend good books for James to read*
  • What I Liked - When I can get it together, I enjoy the mental and intellectual stimulation of books
  • What I Disliked - Being sleepy but having to have a light on so I can see the pages

Avoiding caffeine

"Experts" say not to drink caffeine past 2 pm to get a good nights sleep. I have noticed that if I drink past 11 am I am going to be wired all night. I try to control my caffeine usage and usually I am pretty good at it. Where it becomes an issue is when I didn't get any sleep and I'm tired so I drink caffeine to get me through the day but them I'm up all night again and then I need to drink caffeine the next day to get me through the day but...

Duration Effort Required Consistency
Many months 2/10 - Don't get me started on how literally no one needs caffeine All day, every day
  • Why I Started - Started stopping because I was a bit addicted when working in coffee shops
  • Why I Stopped - Ummm while I haven't exactly completely eliminated it, I am pretty good at controlling my intake
  • What I Liked - I tend to drink more water if I'm not drinking coffee or tea or whatever and hydration is good
  • What I Disliked - Not drinking caffeinated teas all day because I love tea and sometimes that love does not discriminate based on caffeine level

Exercise

I usually stay pretty active. Lately that has involved doing lots of bike riding. In the past (And potentially future if I can handle waking up early) I have been on some pretty good gym schedules. I find that no matter what time I workout at, I become really energetic all day, and well past bed time.

Duration Effort Required Consistency
Umm almost always in the summer, so let's go with that! 6/10 - Not the easiest but like I do love it. Motivation can be tricky, good thing we have our morning workout group! ~4 days a week, give or take
  • Why I Started - I guess I started getting really serious about fitness around my senior year of high school, I'll spare you the story for now
  • Why I Stopped - Can't stop, won't stop
  • What I Liked - #gainz
  • What I Disliked - Being sore all the time I guess lol

Sticking to a consistent sleep schedule

Does not help.

Duration Effort Required Consistency
Depends on where I am employed, but I have been on a consistent schedule since December 2016 because of WOS 5/10 - On one hand it is hard to force yourself to go to sleep at night and not smash the alarm in to a million pieces and continue sleeping in the morning, but I am pretty good at being where I need to be when I need to be there. "To be early is to be on time, to be on time is to be late, to be late is to be dead" or something... ~4 days a week, give or take
  • Why I Started - Because work
  • Why I Stopped - If I stop then that would mean I am unemployed so lets hope that doesn't happen!
  • What I Liked - I guess I kinda dig knowing when I need to be at work no matter what day it is
  • What I Disliked - I hate the sound of alarms

Eliminating naps

I have tried this. The entire first semester of the WOS program I did not take a single nap, weekdays or weekends. I still could not sleep. I love naps.

Duration Effort Required Consistency
Longest I have gone is around 3 months 999/10 - Naps are life Maybe I took one nap on a Sunday during that 3 month period
  • Why I Started - Did not start intentionally, it just sort of happened
  • Why I Stopped - One day I managed to fall asleep after work and I remembered the amazingness I was missing out on
  • What I Liked - Nothing. Pure torture. 0/10 would not do again
  • What I Disliked - Absolutely everything, a punishment I would not wish upon my greatest enemy

Drinking chamomile (and other decaffeinated) tea

This is supposed to help. While I love tea, I have not found it to help. Good news is I'm willing to try to continue drinking tea in efforts to help me sleep because I'll never say no to more tea.

Duration Effort Required Consistency
Geez, that's a tough one... We're going to go with like... Maybe a few years? #specific 10/10 - Because tea Between 1-4 nights a week probably
  • Why I Started - At this time I can not think of where my love for tea began
  • Why I Stopped - Invalid question
  • What I Liked - There are plen-TEA of things to like about tea! =D (You didn't really believe I would make it this far without puns, did you?)
  • What I Disliked - I guess like if I have to answer that, I don't love doing dishes and all those mugs can really add up!

Getting out of bed when unable to sleep

According to the "experts," one should only be in bed when they are sleeping. So I have tried the whole get-out-of-bed-and-do-something-productive-when-you-can't-sleep thing, but that leads to me staying up all night and not getting any sleep at all.

Duration Effort Required Consistency
I try this every once in a blue moon 9/10 - Beds are warm and cozy, everything else is not Nonexistent
  • Why I Started - Read about it in a few places and decided to give it a shot
  • Why I Stopped - It is so awful, trust me
  • What I Liked - While overall it was pretty bad, I guess like spending time doing something useful instead of staring at the ceiling is always a good thing
  • What I Disliked - Getting out of bed and the next day is always unbearable
